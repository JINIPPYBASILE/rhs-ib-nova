Learn More

The IB Diploma Programme at Richmond Secondary School is an academically rigorous, internationally recognized program that requires students to undertake coursework during Grades 11 and 12.

It has a long history at Richmond Secondary School, having been approved by the Richmond School Board in 1984, and located at was then known as Richmond Senior Secondary School. Philosophically, our IB Diploma Programme is inclusive, diverse and welcoming: it is open to all students in Richmond and the Lower Mainland.

Despite the fact that the IB Diploma Programme exposes students to numerous and complex academic demands, in many ways its chief goal is quite simple: to teach students how to learn in any area of knowledge that they choose to pursue.

Over the years we have found that the chief factors that distinguish our very, very best students from our best students do not involve content mastery at all: maturity, responsibility, diligence, perseverance, and humility.

Indeed we have consistently found that our very, very best students are active in the school community and the broader community-at-large prior to enrolling in the IB Diploma Programme and remain active in their communities after enrolling in the IB Diploma Programme.

This is what we mean when we say that we operate a CAS-oriented Diploma Programme.

Part of what IB identifies as the Core, the course called “CAS” is the mechanism by which we encourage and support IB students to be creative, active, and engage in community service during the two years of the Diploma Programme.

It means that rather than being expected to stop participating in school teams, main-stage theater productions, musical concerts, clubs and other interests, IB Diploma Candidates at Richmond Secondary School are expressly expected to continue to pursue these endeavors, as they contribute significantly to so much to a student’s personal, social, emotional and cognitive growth.

As always, it is critical to keep in mind that the IB Diploma Programme simply represents one Graduation pathway available to students at Richmond Secondary School and that achieving academic excellence in the provincial Dogwood Graduation Program is also a challenging undertaking.

About Us

Richmond Secondary is an inclusive public school in Richmond, BC, a suburb of Vancouver, Canada. It is the oldest school in Richmond, having been founded in 1927, and was completely rebuilt in 2004. Our IB Diploma Programme was established in 1984.

Our population is incredibly diverse, consisting of students born in over 40 countries. We have a rich legacy of community involvement, as well as academic and athletic excellence.
